守 Phases

守 Phases presents four different accounts on the practice of ancestral worship. In contemporary times, what are some changes that have impacted this practice? Do they complicate or concretise one’s faith? Do they evolve or erode these practices? Why do we continue to practice?

Ancestral worshipping practices sit in the grey area between culture, religion, and superstition. Because of this, there is a lack of literature on the subject. Instead, most documentations and research tend to focus on the tangible aspects surrounding these practices; such as the rituals and objects used. But the most dynamic facets of these practices lie in how and why people do it. Instances where inherited social and cultural norms are being practiced and questioned. The commitment to continue the spirit and legacy of these practices remains unchanged despite how societal and anthropological change.

These practices are perhaps a reflection of our ephemeral existence and the anxieties surrounding it. By aligning and ascribing to a practice that goes back in time before one’s existence, it helps to establish a greater sense of meaning and identity.

Ancestral worship is not just an act of faith or religion. It is how cultural and familial identity is passed on. It is also a manner of mourning and remembrance for those who have passed.


This work is dedicated to my aunt who left us in 2012.


Hong Shu-ying, 2020
